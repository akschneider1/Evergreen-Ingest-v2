How it works

  1. 1
    Upload two documents. Your source policy (the authority — what should be true) and the document that's supposed to reflect it: a training manual, agent script, FAQ, or knowledge base article.
  2. 2
    Choose what parameters to check. Pick a preset domain — tax or benefits — to load a ready-made schema of parameter types (rates, thresholds, deadlines). Or describe what to look for in plain language.
  3. 3
    Review the drift report. The tool extracts matching parameters from both documents and shows you exactly what's different: same parameter, different value; present in policy but missing from implementation; or the reverse.
  4. 4
    Validate and export. Confirm, edit, or dismiss each finding. Export a report — or push validated parameters directly to your evaluation pipeline or vector store.

Where this fits

  • One review pass. Two outputs. When a policy expert validates findings here, they produce two things at once: a set of confirmed parameters that can be used to test AI systems, and policy-grounded text chunks ready to index into a knowledge base. Review once; both outputs come from the same session.
  • Ready-made test cases for AI evaluation. Each confirmed finding is a complete test case: the correct value from policy, the wrong value from the stale document, and the exact source sentence that proves it. Export to Evergreen Evals or push to Langfuse. Every failed eval traces back to the specific policy text that defines the right answer.
  • Verified content for your knowledge base. Validated parameters carry full lineage — which document, which passage, which expert decision — as metadata. Filter your knowledge base to decision == confirmed to serve only SME-verified content to your AI.
